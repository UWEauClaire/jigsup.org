WIFA Tournament


Jig’s Up host WIFA Ice Fishing Contest

Lake Wissota | February 9, 2019 | 7:30 am – 1 pm


  • 10 person or less High School team, schools may have multiple teams, no sharing of fish with other teams. All WI DNR Fishing regulations apply, https://dnr.wi.gov/.
  • Fishing may only be on Lake Wissota, Chippewa Falls, http://jigsup.org/directions.html.
  • Top Length for each species gets registered (1 of each species): Northern, Bass, Walleye, Perch, Crappie, Bluegill.
  • Use board to measure; each team is responsible to bring in their game fish sheet to the WIFA headquarters at 1 pm signed by your coach. Catch and release is encouraged.
  • Trophies and Prizes awarded to the top teams by total length. Individual prizes for top length in each species.
  • Coaches’ Meeting at 7 am at The View, one coach per school must report to this meeting.
  • $25 per team (max 10 students) payable to UWEC Foundation, bring payment to Coaches’ Meeting
  • Free lunch ticket for all team members: hot dog, chips, and drink
  • Call or E-mail by Thursday, February 5 to reserve a team, include coach’s name, email, and phone number:
    Andy Jepsen | jepsenan@uwec.edu | 715-836-2176

Jig’s Up Blugold Ice Fishing Contest Option:



If any Jig’s Up WIFA Ice Fishing Contest anglers choose to also participate in the Jig’s Up Blugold Ice Fishing Contest and be eligible for the Jig’s Up Blugold Ice Fishing Contest Fish Prizes, they must abide by the Contest rules including:

  • Contest participants may fish only on Lake Wissota on Saturday, February 9, 2019, between 7am and 2pm. Tickets are $25.00 in advance (through February 8) and $40 until 9am on February 9 - with no limit on the number of tickets an individual can purchase or prizes they may win.
  • Anglers must be in line by 2 pm SHARP to register fish. Contestants will be allowed to fish anywhere on Lake Wissota. Ice Fishing Permitted ONLY. One fish per Fish Ticket purchased can be registered of any of the following species: northern, walleye, bass, and pan fish (yellow perch, bluegills, crappies, pumpkinseed, sunfish). Fish not listed here are ineligible for registration. Fish may be taken with tip-ups or by rod and reel. There is no age requirement on contest participation (must be 18 to years of age to claim the truck); anyone over the age of 16 years must hold a valid Wisconsin Fishing license.
  • All fish entered must be alive and brought to a weigh-in station immediately. All fish registered will be marked so they cannot be registered again. Fish that are dead, frozen, or tampered with are ineligible.

For a complete set a rules, visit jigsup.org.
